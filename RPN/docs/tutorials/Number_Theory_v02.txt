# Number Theory Tutorial

Number theory is the study of whole numbers (integers) and their properties.

It is one of the oldest branches of mathematics and forms the foundation of:

* Cryptography
* Computer science
* Prime number research
* Modular arithmetic
* Recreational mathematics

Unlike most areas of mathematics, number theory deals primarily with integers rather than real numbers.

Examples:

2
17
42
99991

---

## Prime Numbers

A prime number is a positive integer greater than 1 that has exactly two divisors:

1
itself

Examples:

2
3
5
7
11
13
17
19

Non-primes are called composite numbers.

Examples:

4 = 2 × 2
6 = 2 × 3
8 = 2 × 4
9 = 3 × 3
10 = 2 × 5

---

## Prime Testing

Prime testing determines whether a number is prime.

Command:

isprime

Example:

RPN> 17 isprime

Result:

1

Meaning:

true

Example:

RPN> 18 isprime

Result:

0

Meaning:

false

Prime testing is one of the most common number theory operations.

---

## Prime Factorization

Every positive integer greater than 1 can be expressed as a product of prime numbers.

Command:

factor

Example:

RPN> 360 factor

Result:

2^3 × 3^2 × 5

because:

360 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 5

This is known as the prime factorization of 360.

A remarkable fact of mathematics is that every positive integer has exactly one prime factorization.

This is called the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic.

---

## Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)

The GCD of two integers is the largest integer that divides both numbers.

Command:

gcd

Example:

RPN> 24 36 gcd

Result:

12

because:

12 divides 24
12 divides 36

and no larger number does.

GCD is useful for:

* Reducing fractions
* Simplifying ratios
* Cryptography
* Modular arithmetic

---

## Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The LCM of two integers is the smallest positive integer that is a multiple of both.

Command:

lcm

Example:

RPN> 12 18 lcm

Result:

36

because:

36 = 12 × 3
36 = 18 × 2

LCM is useful when combining cycles, schedules, or periodic events.

---

## Relationship Between GCD and LCM

For positive integers:

gcd(a,b) × lcm(a,b) = a × b

Example:

gcd(12,18) = 6
lcm(12,18) = 36

6 × 36 = 216
12 × 18 = 216

The identity holds.

## Modular Arithmetic

Modular arithmetic works with remainders.

Command:

mod

Example:

RPN> 17 5 mod

Result:

2

because:

17 = 3 × 5 + 2

The remainder is 2.

Modular arithmetic is the arithmetic of clocks.

Example:

23 + 5 ≡ 4 (mod 24)

because:

28 mod 24 = 4

---

## Integer Division

Integer division discards the remainder.

Command:

idiv

Example:

RPN> 17 5 idiv

Result:

3

because:

17 = 3 × 5 + 2

The remainder is discarded.

## Perfect Numbers

A perfect number equals the sum of its proper divisors.

Example:

6

because:

1 + 2 + 3 = 6

Another example:

28

because:

1 + 2 + 4 + 7 + 14 = 28

Perfect numbers are rare and have fascinated mathematicians for over 2,000 years.

---

## Mersenne Primes

A Mersenne prime has the form:

2^p - 1

where p is prime.

Examples:

3
7
31
127
8191

Large Mersenne primes are often used in record-setting prime searches.

---

## Large Prime Searches

Prime numbers become increasingly rare as numbers grow larger.

Near:

1,000

roughly one number in seven is prime.

Near:

1,000,000

roughly one number in fourteen is prime.

Near:

10^100

primes are much rarer.

Modern prime searches often use:

* Sieving
* Miller-Rabin tests
* Lucas tests
* ECPP proofs

to identify and prove primality.

---

## Why Number Theory Matters

Number theory is far more than a collection of mathematical curiosities.

It powers:

* RSA encryption
* Internet security
* Digital signatures
* Cryptographic key generation
* Error detection algorithms

The security of much of the modern Internet relies on properties of prime numbers and factorization.
