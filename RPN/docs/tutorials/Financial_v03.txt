# Financial Tutorial

The calculator includes several financial functions for solving common time-value-of-money problems.

The most common financial variables are:

    PV     Present Value
    FV     Future Value
    RATE   Interest Rate
    NPER   Number of Periods
    PMT    Payment Per Period

Most financial problems provide four of these values and ask for the fifth.

These functions answer questions such as:

- How much will an investment be worth in the future?
- How much should I invest today to reach a goal?
- How long will it take for an investment to grow?
- What interest rate is required to achieve a target value?
- What payment is required to pay off a loan?

The financial commands are based on the relationship:

    FV = PV × (1 + RATE)^NPER

where:

    PV     Present Value
    FV     Future Value
    RATE   Interest Rate per Period
    NPER   Number of Periods

---

## Present Value (PV)

Present Value answers:

    What is a future amount worth today?

Command:

    pv

The calculator solves for PV when:

    FV
    RATE
    NPER

are known.

Example:

    RPN> 2000
    RPN> 0.05
    RPN> 10
    RPN> pv

Result:

    1227.83

Meaning:

    $1227.83 invested today
    becomes approximately
    $2000 in 10 years
    at 5% interest.

---

## Interest Rate (RATE)

Rate answers:

    What rate of return is required?

Command:

    rate

The calculator solves for RATE when:

    PV
    FV
    NPER

are known.

Example:

    RPN> 1000
    RPN> 2000
    RPN> 10
    RPN> rate

Result:

    0.07177

or approximately:

    7.18%

Meaning:

    An investment must earn
    about 7.18% annually
    to double in 10 years.

---

## Number of Periods (NPER)

Number of Periods answers:

    How long will it take?

Command:

    nper

The calculator solves for NPER when:

    PV
    FV
    RATE

are known.

Example:

    RPN> 1000
    RPN> 2000
    RPN> 0.05
    RPN> nper

Result:

    14.2067

Meaning:

    At 5% growth,
    it takes about
    14.2 years
    to double an investment.

This is one of the most common financial calculations.

    FV
    RATE
    NPER

---

### Example: Mortgage Payment

Suppose:

    Loan Amount: $300,000
    Annual Rate: 6%
    Term: 30 years

Assuming monthly payments:

    PV   = 300000
    FV   = 0
    RATE = 0.06 / 12
    NPER = 30 * 12

Enter:

    RPN> 300000
    RPN> 0
    RPN> 0.005
    RPN> 360
    RPN> pmt

Result:

    1798.65

Meaning:

    A monthly payment of approximately
    $1,798.65 is required.

---

### Example: Retirement Savings

Suppose you wish to accumulate:

    $1,000,000

over:

    30 years

earning:

    7%

and currently have:

    $0

invested.

Enter:

    RPN> 0
    RPN> 1000000
    RPN> 0.07 / 12
    RPN> 360
    RPN> pmt

The result is the monthly contribution required to reach the goal.

This answers:

    How much must I save each month?

## Example: Rule of 72

A common estimate says:

    Years to Double ≈ 72 / Rate%

At:

    5%

the estimate gives:

    72 / 5 = 14.4 years

The calculator gives:

    14.2067 years

which is quite close.

## Periods Matter

The meaning of:

    RATE

depends on the meaning of:

    NPER

Examples:

    Annual:
        RATE = yearly rate
        NPER = years

    Monthly:
        RATE = monthly rate
        NPER = months

    Daily:
        RATE = daily rate
        NPER = days

The units must be consistent.

---

## Percent vs Decimal Rates

Rates should normally be entered as decimals.

Examples:

    5%   -> 0.05
    7%   -> 0.07
    12%  -> 0.12

Common mistake:

    5

instead of:

    0.05

which represents:

    500%

rather than:

    5%

---

## Common Mistakes

### Wrong Rate Format

Incorrect:

    5

Correct:

    0.05

---

### Confusing Annual and Periodic Rates

For monthly payments:

    Annual Rate  = 6%
    Monthly Rate = 0.06 / 12 = 0.005

The rate supplied to the financial functions must match the period represented by NPER.

Examples:

    Monthly payments -> monthly rate
    Annual periods   -> annual rate

---

### Mixing Time Units

Incorrect:

    Annual rate
    Monthly periods

Correct:

    Annual rate with years

or

    Monthly rate with months

---

### Solving For The Wrong Variable

Remember:

    pv

solves for Present Value.

    fv

solves for Future Value.

    rate

solves for Interest Rate.

    nper

solves for Number of Periods.

    pmt

solves for Payment Per Period.

Be sure the desired unknown is the command you invoke.
